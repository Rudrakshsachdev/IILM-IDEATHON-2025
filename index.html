Judgment Criteria

Your idea will be evaluated based on these key properties

Novelty

Is the project innovative or unique? Does it bring a fresh perspective to the problem?

Usefulness

Does it address key needs effectively? How practical is the solution?

Flexibility

Can it adapt to changes? How well does it accommodate different scenarios?

Scalability & Impact

Can it grow and benefit a larger audience? What is its potential reach?

Sustainability

Is it viable long-term across various factors? Economic, environmental, and social sustainability.

General Instructions & Requirements

Important information for all participants

General Instructions

  • Participants must arrive at the venue at least 30 minutes before the event starts
  • The event will follow a strict schedule, so participants should adhere to the timeline provided
  • Any form of plagiarism or idea duplication will lead to disqualification
  • Participants can form teams before the event or at the networking session during registration
  • Each team must nominate a team leader to communicate with the organizers
  • All team members must actively participate in brainstorming, discussions, and presentations
  • Participants must maintain professionalism and respect toward fellow participants, mentors, and judges
  • Any form of misconduct will result in disqualification
  • Decisions made by the jury will be final and binding

Poster Requirements

  • Poster must be printed on flex only
  • Flex size should be 4 Ft (width) * 3 Ft (Height)
  • Design must focus on Idea, Prototype, Features, Novelty, Applications, Methodology, and Market Potential
  • Design must have very little text content, and use images, diagrams, Tables etc to explain the idea
  • Participants must manage their posters at their own cost
  • Participants are required to bring threads and clips for display setup
  • Sample design template: Canva Link

Idea Confirmation Points

  • Problem-Solution Fit: Does the idea solve a real and significant problem?
  • Market Validation: Is there a demand, and how unique is the solution compared to competitors?
  • Feasibility: Can it be implemented with available skills, resources, and budget?
  • Scalability: Can the idea grow and adapt for larger markets or different audiences?
  • Risk Analysis: What are the potential challenges, and how can they be mitigated?

Frequently Asked Questions

Find answers to common questions about IILM Ideathon 2025

Who can participate in IILM Ideathon 2025?

IILM Ideathon 2025 is open to all students from any recognized university or college in India. The event welcomes undergraduate and postgraduate students, as well as young entrepreneurs and tech enthusiasts.

What is the registration fee for the event?

There is a nominal fee of ₹299 per team for IILM Ideathon 2025.

What should be included in our presentation?

Your presentation should focus on your idea, prototype, features, novelty, applications, methodology, and market potential. Use visual elements like images, diagrams, and tables rather than extensive text content.

What are the poster requirements?

Posters must be printed on flex material with dimensions of 4 feet (width) × 3 feet (height). They should visually represent your idea with minimal text, using images, diagrams, and tables to explain the concept.

Can I participate as an individual?

While we encourage team participation to foster collaboration, individual entries are also accepted. However, teams have a competitive advantage in terms of diverse skills and perspectives.

What resources will be provided during the event?

We will provide basic prototyping materials, mentorship from industry experts, networking opportunities, and refreshments. Participants should bring their own laptops and any specialized equipment they might need.
